Overview

This portfolio demonstrates risk-based Cyber Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) thinking in a simulated SaaS environment. It focuses on identifying, assessing, and managing cyber risks impacting customer data, emphasising governance, proportional controls, and defensible decision-making rather than checklist compliance.

Focus: customer data risk, SaaS operating realities, and practical governance aligned to recognised frameworks (ISO 27001 and NIST CSF).

SaaS Environment Context

This portfolio is based on a simulated mid-size SaaS organisation providing a cloud-based customer relationship management (CRM) platform.

Organisation Profile

  • Approximately 200 employees
  • B2B SaaS organisation operating in the UK, EU, and US
  • Cloud-hosted environment
  • Processes customer personal data (PII)

Technology Overview

  • Cloud infrastructure hosting application and data
  • Centralised identity and access management
  • Third-party service integrations
  • Logging and monitoring capabilities

Key Business Priorities

  • Protection of customer data
  • Service availability and reliability
  • Regulatory compliance (e.g. data protection requirements)

Risk Identification Methodology

Cyber risks were identified using a structured, risk-based methodology grounded in the defined SaaS business and technology context, rather than through exhaustive threat catalogues or vulnerability-driven analysis.

Methodology Overview

  • Defined business scope, operating model, and regulatory exposure
  • Identified critical assets, with particular focus on customer personal data
  • Analysed high-risk system components such as identity and access management, cloud infrastructure, and third-party integrations
  • Developed risk statements by combining assets, plausible threat scenarios, and realistic weaknesses
  • Prioritised risks based on potential business impact and likelihood in a mid-size SaaS environment

This approach ensured focus on material risks that would reasonably concern business and security stakeholders, rather than attempting to enumerate all possible threats.

Use of Frameworks

ISO 27001 and the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (NIST CSF) were used as reference frameworks to:

  • Validate that identified risks aligned with recognised security domains
  • Support consistent assessment of control coverage
  • Inform proportional and defensible control selection

Frameworks were applied as guidance rather than prescriptive checklists.

Approach to Cyber GRC

This portfolio reflects a pragmatic approach to Cyber GRC, balancing security risk reduction with operational practicality in a mid-size SaaS organisation.

  • Prioritisation of material risks over exhaustive threat catalogues
  • Selection of proportionate, defensible controls
  • Clear ownership and accountability for risk treatment
  • Alignment with business priorities and regulatory expectations

Notes

  • This portfolio is based on a simulated scenario and does not represent a real organisation.
  • AI tools were used for drafting and structuring content; all risk assessments, control selections, and judgement decisions were reviewed and refined manually.
