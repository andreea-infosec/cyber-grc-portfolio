Overview

This portfolio demonstrates risk-based Cyber Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) thinking in a simulated SaaS environment. It focuses on identifying, assessing, and managing cyber risks impacting customer data, emphasising governance, proportional controls, and defensible decision-making rather than checklist compliance.

Focus: customer data risk, SaaS operating realities, and practical governance aligned to recognised frameworks (ISO 27001 and NIST CSF).

SaaS Environment Context

This portfolio is based on a simulated mid-size SaaS organization providing a cloud-based customer relationship management (CRM) platform.

Organization Profile

  • Approximately 200 employees
  • B2B SaaS organization operating in the UK, EU, and US
  • Cloud-hosted environment
  • Processes customer personal data (PII)

Technology Overview

  • Cloud infrastructure hosting application and data
  • Centralized identity and access management
  • Third-party service integrations
  • Logging and monitoring capabilities

Key Business Priorities

  • Protection of customer data
  • Service availability and reliability
  • Regulatory compliance (e.g. data protection requirements)

This context is used to scope risk identification, control assessment, and recommended actions throughout the portfolio.

Approach to Cyber GRC

This portfolio applies a pragmatic, risk-based approach to Cyber GRC, prioritising material risks and proportionate controls. Frameworks are used as guidance rather than prescriptive checklists.

  • Prioritisation of material risks over exhaustive threat catalogues
  • Selection of proportionate, defensible controls
  • Clear ownership and accountability
  • Use of recognised frameworks as guidance (ISO 27001 and NIST CSF)

Decisions were made to balance security risk reduction with operational practicality in a mid-size SaaS context.

Notes

  • This portfolio is based on a simulated scenario and does not represent a real organisation.
  • AI tools were used for drafting and structuring content; all risk assessments and decisions were reviewed and refined manually.
