Cyber Risk Assessment – SaaS Environment

1. Background

This case study presents a simulated cyber risk assessment conducted for a mid-size SaaS organization providing a cloud-based CRM platform.

2. Scope and Assumptions

The assessment focused on risks impacting customer data processed within the SaaS platform, including identity and access management, third-party integrations, and cloud infrastructure.

The assessment is based on a hypothetical scenario and is intended to demonstrate Cyber GRC thinking rather than represent a real organization.

3. Risk Assessment Methodology

Risks were identified and assessed using a qualitative risk-based approach, considering likelihood and business impact. Existing controls were reviewed to determine control effectiveness and residual risk.

4. Key Risks Identified

  • Credential compromise due to phishing
  • Third-party vendor security breach
  • Cloud infrastructure misconfiguration

5. Control Environment Overview

The organization has implemented baseline security controls including multi-factor authentication, logging and monitoring, vendor due diligence, and cloud configuration management processes.

6. Gaps and Observations

While foundational controls are in place, several areas were identified where governance and assurance could be strengthened, including consistency of access reviews, ongoing vendor oversight, and monitoring of cloud configuration changes.

Evidence Considerations

Control effectiveness would be supported through evidence such as access review records, security awareness training completion reports, authentication and logging records, vendor risk assessment documentation, and cloud configuration review outputs.

These artifacts would be reviewed periodically as part of assurance or audit activities to validate ongoing control operation.

7. Recommended Actions

Recommendations were focused on reducing residual risk through proportionate, risk-based improvements, including security awareness training, formalised review processes, and enhanced monitoring capabilities.

8. Key Judgement Calls

  • Limiting the risk register to three risks: Focused on material risks impacting customer data rather than exhaustive threat coverage.
  • Maintaining a medium residual risk rating: Reflected realistic likelihood of control failure without overstating assurance.
  • Avoiding mandatory periodic password rotation: Prioritised phishing resistance and detection given MFA implementation.
  • Prioritising governance improvements over additional technical controls: Emphasised consistency, accountability, and monitoring rather than introducing new tools.

9. Lessons Learned

This assessment highlighted the importance of applying a risk-based approach rather than relying solely on prescriptive controls or framework checklists.

It reinforced the value of clearly defining scope, prioritising material risks, and documenting ownership and accountability. The process demonstrated that effective cyber risk management depends as much on governance and consistency as on technical controls.
